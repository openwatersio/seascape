# Land clamp redesign + unsurveyed inland water — planning doc
*Written 2026-07-07. Point-in-time; the code is the source of truth.*

## Goal

Stop the land clamp from erasing real bathymetry in tidal rivers, make trusted sources structurally immune to land-mask errors, and render inland water we have no bathymetry for as honest unsurveyed water instead of land. Four pieces: a sign-flip guard in the merge feather (which lets `clamp_merged` be deleted), inland-water subtraction in the mask rasterize, an unsurveyed-water tileset, and drying re-gated by the same mask change.

## What's broken (verified 2026-07-07, production tiles)

- **ICW at Jacksonville** (30.36125, −81.46539): S-102 has the dredged channel (1.21 m below MLLW, tile `102US004FL2QE2622D7`) and CUDEM ninth covers the whole corridor (channel −0.54 m NAVD88, marsh positive). Production renders flat 0.0 across the channel. Mechanism: `landmask.clamp_merged` clamps the *final merged DEM* to 0 wherever the OSM land mask says land, and OSM land polygons put the ICW inside the coastline (mask=1 on the channel; the St. Johns main stem is mask=0 and renders fine).
- **Elbe through Hamburg**: production transect at 9.93°E reads flat 0.0 across the main fairway. EMODnet is flagged `land_clamp` and OSM's coastline closure is downstream, so the *per-source* clamp erases real German waterway surveys before the merge even runs — nothing unflagged covers the Elbe, so fixing `clamp_merged` alone doesn't restore it.
- **Amazon at Macapá**: flat 0.0 across the ~20 km north channel; GEBCO's charted depths there are zeroed by the same per-source clamp.
- Not broken: the Caspian survives (OSM maps it as coastline → mask=0); shallow lakes are sign-inert (beds above MSL render as land under the elevation<0 water rule regardless — that's why lake sources carry surface offsets); Great Lakes et al. are covered by dedicated unflagged sources. The Dead Sea/Salton stay flattened — accepted ceiling, dedicated-source path if ever wanted. Deep cryptodepression lakes are the exception — see Part 2's ceiling.
- BlueTopo is *not* the fix for any of this: it has the identical ribbon-only NBS coverage as S-102 (−2.06 m MLLW in the ICW channel, nodata over the marsh) plus the datum work S-102 already solved.

Two distinct design flaws:

1. `land_clamp` is per-source policy, but `clamp_merged` enforces it a second time after the merge, where source identity is gone — so it clamps by geography and damages trusted sources. The landmask docstring's "S-102/CUDEM can never be damaged" promise is broken by its own module.
2. The clamp mask (pure OSM land polygons) is too fat: it swallows tidal rivers where the *flagged* sources hold genuine data (Elbe, Amazon).

## Design rules

- **Source rule**: source-scoped policy runs while pixels still have source identity — at warp time, never after the merge.
- **Stage rule**: no downstream stage may *manufacture* water — a pixel entering a stage ≥ 0 or nodata must not leave it negative. `smooth.smooth_array` already obeys (`np.where(water, out, dem)` — land and nodata untouched); the merge seam feather is the one violator.
- **Honesty rule**: where OSM says water and no source measured depth, render unsurveyed water — never land, never fabricated depth. This makes OSM mask errors cosmetic instead of destructive, which is what lets the first two rules stay simple.

## Part 1 — feather guard; delete clamp_merged

- `aggregation_merge.py`: in the feather block, keep a pre-feather copy of the window taken **after** the `merged_tile[merged_tile == NODATA] = 0` fill — a truly-uncovered pixel must enter the guard as 0, else a 0-filled nodata pixel that feathers negative escapes a ≥0 predicate and fabricates a water rim around data holes. After the smoothstep blend, restore any pixel that flipped from ≥ 0 to < 0 to its pre-feather value. One `np.where`. Land keeps its true topo (today's `clamp_merged` flattened bled pixels to 0; for GEBCO-clamped land the pre-value is 0 anyway, for trusted land it's real elevation — strictly better).
- Delete `landmask.clamp_merged` and its call site in `aggregation_run.run`. The per-tile `landmask.tif` itself **stays** — `aggregation_reproject` still rasterizes it for the per-source clamp, and `drying_run.drying_mask` consumes it.
- Rewrite the landmask module docstring: the two-clamp-points paragraph goes away, and "flagged sources only" becomes structurally true instead of promised.
- Test: synthetic land/water seam through merge (feather) + smooth; assert no ≥0/nodata pixel comes out negative.

## Part 2 — subtract inland water from the clamp mask

- Add an inland-water polygon set alongside `land.fgb` and burn it as a second pass in `landmask.rasterize`: burn land=1, then burn water=0 over it. No planet-scale vector erase — the subtraction happens per-tile in the raster, same `-te`/`-tr` seam contract, still deterministic. `landmask.py prep-water` (`just watermask`) builds the water mask; the burn is skipped when the water file is absent (mask degrades to today's behavior, not a crash).
- Effect: flagged sources keep their genuine negative data inside mapped water — EMODnet's Elbe fairway, GEBCO's Amazon channel. Worst case inside a real water polygon is a coarse depth where water truly exists; the old worst case was land where water exists.
- **These depths ship provisional and biased deep.** GEBCO is ~MSL, which sits ~1–3 m above chart datum in the macrotidal estuaries this unclamps (Amazon range 4 m+ with a bore); EMODnet's LAT datum is unverified ("LAT (confirm)" in the source table) — if it's really MSL at Hamburg (range ~3.6 m, tidally amplified up-estuary) the fairway reads ~2 m too deep. Deeper-than-reality is the unsafe direction for a chart, so Part 3's provenance-gated confidence signal is a ship-together requirement, not a nice-to-have. Per-source bias for the record: S-102 ≈ correct (MLLW); CUDEM ~0.95 m too deep (NAVD88, see Part 4); GEBCO/MSL ~1–3 m too deep in macrotidal estuaries; EMODnet unknown until confirmed.
- **Wetlands stay in the mask.** They are above-datum terrain; flagged-source negatives there are exactly the shoreline-straddle junk the clamp exists for.
- Feed (verified 2026-07-07 against the live bucket): Overture Maps water as GeoParquet (OSM-derived, ODbL like the land polygons we already attribute) — it lives under `theme=base/type=water` (there is no `theme=water`), and GDAL opens the partition directory as one ~65M-feature dataset, anonymously. Two implementation traps, both hit and fixed: geometry-type predicates (`OGR_GEOMETRY`, in `-where` or `-sql`) silently match *nothing* through GDAL's Arrow read path, so dropping river/stream centerlines takes a second, local pass (SQLite dialect, `GeometryType(...) LIKE '%POLYGON%'`); and GDAL honors `AWS_DEFAULT_REGION`, not `AWS_REGION` — it must be pinned to `us-west-2` or a dev/CI profile aimed at another S3-compatible store (region `auto`) poisons the bucket hostname.
- **Coverage ceiling (verified): this only reopens water OSM maps as polygons — which covers navigation channels, not every marsh creek.** Tested end-to-end through the new `rasterize` with exact containment checks: the Elbe fairway, the Alster, and the ICW's main dredged Pablo Creek channel (S-102 shows 6.8–9.8 m there) all flip to water in the mask. What stays land-side is the fringe: narrow intertidal marsh creeks (OSM maps the marsh as `wetland=marsh` plus bare `waterway` centerlines — e.g. the creek slivers around 30.361, −81.465 where S-102 carries thin 1.2 m ribbons). Harmless for the clamp (creek bathymetry is trusted-source data Part 1 already protects), but drying and Part 3's `water` class stay gated in those creeks until OSM area-maps them or another feed (e.g. NHD for US waters) is added. Beware bbox-vs-containment when spot-checking any of this: parquet `-spat` pushdown is bbox-only, and water polygons have island holes — verify with `ST_Contains`, not point-near-polygon.
- Known ceiling to note in code: **cryptodepression lakes** — surface above MSL, bed below — newly expose coarse GEBCO "depths" referenced to nothing a mariner can use: Baikal (bed −1186 m), Tanganyika, Malawi, Ladoga, Onega, Great Slave, Great Bear, Vänern — several navigable, none with a dedicated source yet. Shallow lakes stay sign-inert (beds above MSL render as land). The backstops are dedicated lake sources and Part 3's provenance-gated confidence signal — DEM-sign show-through alone would be overprinted by exactly these depths.

## Part 3 — unsurveyed inland-water tileset

The honest-rendering half (Brandon's proposal): merge OSM water/tidalflat polygons into the published output as unknown-depth features. Strictly this is an ENC **M_COVR/CATCOV=2** analog (a depth-coverage gap — a survey may exist, we just hold no depths); UNSARE (never surveyed) is only the closest *visual* precedent, and S-52 has no rich standardized unsurveyed fill to borrow — historically it's the absence of soundings plus an "Unsurveyed" legend — so the treatment is ours to design.

- One vector layer `unsurveyed` with a `class` attribute: `water` (rivers/lakes/canals) and `tidalflat` (`wetland=tidalflat`, `natural=mud`). **Wetlands are a separate land-side class, never unsurveyed water**: a marsh or mangrove is vegetated land in S-57 terms, and tinting it as water invites reading navigable water where there is none. Same source data as Part 2's subtraction feed — build both from one download.
- **Intertidal marsh/mangrove scope (verified 2026-07-07 — DEFERRED: a separate step after everything else in this plan ships):** paper charts render exactly this — INT1 marsh and mangrove symbols, NOAA's green-with-grass-tufts tint — and the data exists. Overture carries OSM wetlands under `theme=base/type=land`, `subtype='wetland'` (the base/land partition is ~74M features planet-wide, wetlands a filtered subset); the ICW marsh polygon that started this whole investigation contains our test point. One trap: the Overture `class` column flattens everything to `wetland` — the kind lives in the `source_tags` JSON column (`{"natural":"wetland","wetland":"marsh"}`, plus sparse `tidal=yes`/`salt=yes`), verified at both the ICW (marsh/tidalflat/swamp) and the Wadden Sea (tidalflat/saltmarsh/reedbed). So the prep pass classifies off `json_extract(source_tags,'$.wetland')`: `tidalflat`/`mud` → the `tidalflat` class; `saltmarsh`/`mangrove`/`marsh` → a `marsh` class (chart marsh symbology; `marsh` alone doesn't guarantee tidal, but the marsh symbol is not tidal-specific, so mis-including a freshwater marsh is cosmetically harmless); `swamp`/`bog`/`fen`/`reedbed` → skip. Volumes (taginfo, 2026-07): ~4.5M `natural=wetland` polygons planet-wide, of which the keep-set is ~0.67M (marsh 520k + mangrove 58k + tidalflat 46k + saltmarsh 42k) — an order of magnitude smaller than the water feed, so no tileset-budget concern. ~2M carry no `wetland=*` kind at all; they're skipped initially (half the tagged ones are bog/swamp, so guessing marsh would be wrong as often as right) — a coastal-proximity heuristic could reclaim coastal ones later. Wetlands stay OUT of the clamp mask regardless (above-datum terrain — unchanged). This upgrades the ICW marsh from blank land to chart-style marsh even before OSM ever polygon-maps its creeks.
- Own small tileset (`unsurveyed.pmtiles`), following the coverage-pmtiles pattern exactly ([coverage-pmtiles.md](coverage-pmtiles.md)): separate archive, own zoom range, Worker TileJSON + tile route, style source. It cannot live in `vector.pmtiles` for the same zoom-range-trap reasons.
- Zoom: rivers are narrow, so z8 is too coarse — start at maxzoom ~z10–11 (z10's 4096 MVT grid resolves ~10 m) with tippecanoe simplification, knob like `COVERAGE_MAX_ZOOM`. Measure the archive size before committing; planet inland water is much bigger than source footprints.
- Style: render beneath the depth layers — consistent with S-52, which puts meta/coverage areas under DEPARE and soundings — so measured data wins visually. But show-through-by-DEM-sign is **not sufficient**: Part 2 deliberately makes the DEM read water in coarse-source rivers, which would overprint this layer exactly where honesty matters most (a confident-looking GEBCO channel drawn over the Amazon). So pair it with a **provenance-gated confidence signal**: the coverage layer gains a CATZOC-analog attribute (`zoc`, low for `land_clamp` sources), and the style down-weights or overlays coarse-source depth in inland water off it. Coverage's `source_id` answers *who measured this*; `zoc` answers *how much to trust the number* — both ENC concepts (M_QUAL/CATZOC), both needed. Exact layer order and fill treatment get worked out against [depth-areas.md](depth-areas.md) when implementing.

## Part 4 — drying follows the mask

`drying_run.drying_mask` gates on elevation in [0, DRYING_CAP] AND `mask == 0` and reuses the same per-tile `landmask.tif` — so Part 2's water burn automatically extends DEM-derived drying into inland tidal waters with no drying-code change, *where OSM maps them as water polygons* (see Part 2's coverage ceiling: main channels like the Elbe fairway and the ICW's dredged cut qualify; unmapped marsh creeks don't). Notes:

- Limitation, not just a watch item: the gate has no concept of the **head of tide**. Above the tidal limit, water level is river stage, not tide — a bar exposed at low flow is not a drying flat, and green tint falsely implies covers/uncovers-with-the-tide timing a mariner would plan against. The [0, cap] gate confines the damage to near-sea-level reaches (a 400 m lakeshore can't tint), but that's exactly where the big mixed tidal/fluvial rivers live (Amazon, Elbe, Columbia, St. Johns). Principled fix if a tidal-limit dataset ever lands: gate drying to below the estuarine tidal limit.
- Datum: NAVD88 zero sits ~0.95 m **above** MLLW at Jacksonville (Mayport 8720211: NAVD88 = +0.947 m re MLLW), so un-shifted CUDEM reads ~0.95 m too **low/deep** relative to chart datum. A flat that truly dries to +0.5 m MLLW stores as −0.45 m and classifies as navigable water: drying flats are *missed* and shallow depths *overstated* — the unsafe, non-conservative direction for a chart. The chart-datum work ([#16](https://github.com/openwatersio/seascape/issues/16)) must **add** the separation, and it's spatially varying (NAVD88 falls *below* MLLW in parts of the Pacific NW), so a scalar `source_datum --offset` can't fix CUDEM — it needs VDatum-class surfaces. The `tidalflat` class in Part 3 is the no-data fallback rendering, not a substitute.
- `DRYING_GROW_PX`/`DRYING_SEAWARD_PX` are sized to open-coast coastline↔DEM registration error; in a river a few pixels wide the same grow can claim the whole channel. Preview specifically on narrow inland water.

## Rejected alternatives

- **Restore BlueTopo**: identical NBS ribbon coverage to S-102, plus the datum work S-102 obsoleted. Gains nothing anywhere.
- **Subtract trusted-source valid-data footprints from the mask before `clamp_merged`**: works, but keeps the wrongly-scoped post-merge clamp and adds per-merge footprint bookkeeping. The feather guard achieves the invariant by deletion.
- **Per-pixel provenance band through the merge**: also achieves source-scoped clamping post-merge, but is a heavier merge change for the same result the guard gets for one line — and the guard also fixes bleed the provenance approach can't (a bled pixel's provenance is still the land source).
- **Pre-clamp GEBCO at ingest to nodata-on-land**: kills land topo in the terrain product (the tiles are a land+water DEM) — a product-scope change smuggled into a bug fix.
- **One "perfect" mask serving clamp + drying + rendering**: the consumers have different error budgets (destroyed data vs. mis-tint vs. mis-label); Part 2/3/4 deliberately give each its own failure mode instead.
- **Inverse mask — drop the land polygons, mask = NOT(water)**: technically possible with one feed (Overture water *including* `subtype=ocean` is the same OSM-coastline derivation as the osmdata land product, so `-init 1` + burn water=0 reproduces today's semantics), and inland the two schemes are identical — both only reopen water OSM maps as polygons. Rejected for two reasons. Failure-mode asymmetry: the clamp is destructive, and it should key on *positive evidence of land* (a mapped polygon), not on the *absence* of water — a missing/broken water feed in the inverse scheme reads the ocean as land and flattens GEBCO's entire sea to 0, where a missing land polygon today costs a cosmetic rim, and the safe degrade-to-land-only behavior (absent `water.fgb`, guarded CI export) inverts into flatten-the-planet. And the land polygons' edge is load-bearing cartography: OSM coastline is mapped at (mean) high water by convention, which is exactly the high-water shoreline `drying_run` needs (drying = [0, cap] seaward of it) — the most consistently-surveyed line in OSM, not worth trading for one fewer download.

## Rollout

Code/mask-only changes mark nothing dirty in the incremental build (the covering hashes source sets, not code): clear the R2 aggregation + contour state (and the drying/depare forks) before the rebuild that ships this, or the planet stays a patchwork of old-clamp and new-clamp tiles. Same caveat as the contour-level changes.

CI builds and serves the water mask alongside the land mask: the `landmask` job runs `just watermask` and uploads `water.fgb` next to `land.fgb`, both under one recipe-hash on `landmask.py` (so they never mirror different snapshots of the module), and `aggregate` exports `WATERMASK` at the R2 copy — but only when the object exists, so a build against pre-water R2 state degrades to land-only instead of failing every tile's clip. `just watermask` stays for local runs. Same force-dispatch caveat as the land mask above: the covering can't see the mask, so refreshing it still needs a `force=true` build to re-aggregate the flagged tiles.
