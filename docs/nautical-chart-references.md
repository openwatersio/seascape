# Nautical-Chart Cartography — Reference Material

Primary sources on how nautical charts represent depth (soundings, contours, safety
depth, datums) and how bathymetry is generalized for charting. Compiled for the
chart-data-model work (chart datum [#16](https://github.com/openwatersio/seascape/issues/16), soundings/safety-depth, confidence & provenance [#17](https://github.com/openwatersio/seascape/issues/17)); useful to any
agent touching `pipelines/soundings_run.py`, `pipelines/contour_run.py`, datum handling,
or the viewer's chart styling.

**Verification status** — each source is marked:

- ✅ **read** — the primary document was fetched and read; direct quotes below are verbatim from it.
- ◑ **citation-verified** — bibliographic details confirmed (Crossref / publisher / open abstract), but the full text was **not** accessed here (paywall or bot-block, noted); claims are from the abstract or corroborated by a ✅ source.

## Actionable takeaways (each backed by a source below)

- **Round to the shoaler side, always.** Soundings round down, drying heights round up (IHO S-4 B-412 ✅). We floor depth toward shallower — keep it.
- **Shoal-biased selection.** Generalization must never let a mariner interpolate a depth _deeper_ than the source shows — IHO S-4's "triangular method" (Triangle + Edge tests) ✅; Zoraster & Bayer select prime and background soundings "with a shoal bias" ✅.
- **Density widens with depth; background pattern is a regular lattice.** Zoraster & Bayer, verbatim: background soundings "present a **regular pattern** … **triangular or rectangular** with the **pacing between soundings increasing with depth**" ✅ (a quincunx/rhomboidal lattice is exactly this). IHO S-4 B-410d: flat areas get few, evenly-spaced soundings "gradually becoming more widely spaced as the depth increases" ✅. NOAA's Nautical Chart Manual quantifies legible spacing at chart scale — **critical ≥ 6 mm apart, supporting ≥ 10 mm, fill 15–30 mm** (quoted verbatim in Skopeliti et al. 2020 ✅).
- **Zoom-out drops the deeper soundings first.** ENC does this with **SCAMIN** — a per-feature minimum-display-scale attribute (S-57 §2.2.7 ✅) ≈ our per-feature tippecanoe `minzoom`. Assign it shoal-biased.
- **True position is the chart rule.** Soundings sit at their surveyed location; paper charts allow out-of-position _least-depth_ soundings only, flagged with a pointer/brackets (S-4 B-412 ✅); **ENC forbids out-of-position entirely** — "There is no 'sounding, out of position' in an ENC" (S-57 UOC B-412.2 ✅). A jittered/quincunx grid is a raster-derived "background sounding" approximation, not chart-authentic prime selection.
- **Radius method, ~free via tippecanoe.** Pre-sort soundings shoalest-first, then `--drop-densest-as-needed` (spacing grows as needed) + `-pi` (preserve input order) keeps the shoalest and thins the dense deeper fill — the classic radius method, densifying with zoom (tippecanoe docs ✅; mechanism matches Zoraster "eliminate nearby soundings to avoid overcrowding" ✅ and Skopeliti's "buffer radius" ✅).
- **Keep-shoalest on label collision.** MapLibre `symbol-sort-key = depth` (ascending) + `text-allow-overlap: false` → shallowest placed first, wins the collision (style spec ✅). We already do this.
- **Safety depth (feeds the viewer's safety-depth shading).** S-52 emphasizes soundings ≤ the mariner-set safety depth and highlights the safety contour (◑).

## A. Primary standards (IHO / NOAA / NGA)

- ✅ **IHO S-4 — Regulations for INT Charts and Chart Specifications**, Ed. 4.9.0, Mar 2021. Part B §400, regs **B-410…B-413**: shoal-biased triangular selection, density by seabed type, least-depth rules, true-position placement, shoaler-side rounding. https://iho.int/uploads/user/pubs/standards/s-4/S4_V4-9-0_March_2021.pdf
- ✅ **IHO S-57 Appendix B.1 Annex A — Use of the Object Catalogue (UOC)**, Ed. 4.3.0, Oct 2022. `SOUNDG` (multipoint), **`SCAMIN`** (§2.2.7, minimum display scale / decluttering), `EXPSOU` (shoaler-than-surrounding), "there is no 'sounding, out of position' in an ENC" (B-412.2). https://iho.int/uploads/user/pubs/standards/s-57/S-57%20Appendix%20B.1%20Annex%20A_UOC_Ed%204.3.0_Final.pdf · base standard Ed. 3.1.0 https://iho.int/uploads/user/pubs/standards/s-57/31Main.pdf
- ◑ **IHO S-52 — Chart Content and Display Aspects of ECDIS** (+ Presentation Library), Ed. 6.1.1, Jun 2015. PDF confirmed live; safety-depth emphasis of soundings + safety-contour highlighting from the spec summary (not deep-read here). https://iho.int/uploads/user/pubs/standards/s-52/S-52%20Edition%206.1.1%20-%20June%202015.pdf
- ✅ **US Chart No. 1** (NOAA + NGA) — Section I "Depths": sounding symbols, out-of-position sounding, drying heights, doubtful-quality markers (SD/ED/Rep/PA/PD). https://nauticalcharts.noaa.gov/publications/docs/us-chart-1/ChartNo1.pdf
- ✅ **NOAA Nautical Chart User's Manual**, 1st ed. 1997. Shoal-bias selection, chart-user facing: soundings notably shoaler/deeper than their surroundings are treated as "important" and usually selected; a shoal's least depth is charted first, then "supportive soundings and curves are then selected to reinforce this least depth." https://repository.library.noaa.gov/view/noaa/49555
- ◑ **NOAA Nautical Chart Manual, Vol. 1 — Policies and Procedures** — the internal production manual (distinct from the User's Manual above). Its legible sounding-spacing figures (critical ≥ 6 mm, supporting ≥ 10 mm, fill 15–30 mm at chart scale) are quoted verbatim in Skopeliti et al. 2020 ✅; the manual's own PDF bot-blocks automated fetch. Landing: https://repository.library.noaa.gov/view/noaa/49956

## B. Sounding-selection algorithms (cartographic literature)

- ✅ **Zoraster, S. & Bayer, S. (1992), "Automated cartographic sounding selection,"** International Hydrographic Review LXIX(1), March 1992 (open-access reprint, pp. 103ff). Origin of the **radius method**, developed for the US National Ocean Service (NOAA/NOS). Classifies soundings **prime / background / limiting**; prime soundings "deviate significantly in depth from the bottom depth which would be expected based on linear interpolation between chart depth curves" and are "selected with a shoal bias"; background soundings form a regular triangular/rectangular lattice with spacing increasing with depth; as soundings are chosen, "nearby soundings … are eliminated from further consideration to avoid overcrowding." Read from the scan at https://journals.lib.unb.ca/index.php/ihr/article/download/23255/27030/0
- ✅ **Du, J., Lu, Y. & Zhai, J. (2001), "A Model of Sounding Generalization Based on Recognition of Terrain Features,"** Proc. 20th ICC, Beijing. TIN + "sounding tree"; adopts prime/background/limiting; depth-scaled background spacing (1–1.5 cm <20 m … up to 3 cm >50 m); always keep deepest+shallowest per subarea. https://icaci.org/files/documents/ICC_proceedings/ICC2001/icc2001/file/f04006.pdf
- ✅ **Peters, R.Y., Ledoux, H. & Meijers, B.M. (2013), "Generation and generalization of safe depth-contours for hydrographic charts using a surface-based approach,"** Proc. 16th ICA Generalisation Workshop, Dresden (TU Delft). The four constraints (attributed to Zhang & Guilbert 2011) — **safety, legibility, topology, morphology**; safety, verbatim: "At every location, the indicated depth may never be deeper than the depth that was originally measured at that location"; contours may be moved only toward greater depth. Voronoi natural-neighbour surface → extract safe contours. https://gdmc.nl/publications/2013/Safe_depth-contours_hydrographic_charts.pdf
- ◑ **Dyer, N., Kastrisios, C. & De Floriani, L. (2022), "Label-based generalization of bathymetry data for hydrographic sounding selection,"** Cartography and GIS 49(4). **Label-based** selection — thin by the printed label's footprint so labels don't over-plot; shoal-biased; best on safety+legibility vs radius/grid. Abstract read; full text paywalled. DOI 10.1080/15230406.2021.2014974 · https://scholars.unh.edu/ccom/1505/ · open-source tool https://github.com/NoelDyer/Sounding-Selection
- ◑ **Kastrisios, C., Calder, B. et al. (2019), "Towards automated validation of charted soundings,"** Geo-spatial Information Science. Triangle test + Edge test for shoal-bias validation; proposes the Nautical Surface Test. Abstract read; full text paywalled. DOI 10.1080/10095020.2019.1618636 · https://scholars.unh.edu/ccom/1736/
- ◑ **Guilbert, E. (2016), "Feature-Driven Generalization of Isobaths on Nautical Charts: A Multi-Agent System Approach,"** Transactions in GIS 20(2) (Crossref-verified title/venue/year). Generalizes **depth contours** (not point soundings); safety imposes a deformation direction — a contour may move only toward the **shoaler** side. Relevant to contour smoothing (M4.3). Paywalled. DOI 10.1111/tgis.12147
- ✅ **Skopeliti, A., Stamou, L. & Tsoulos, L. (2020), "Generalization of Soundings across Scales: From DTM to Harbour and Approach Nautical Charts,"** ISPRS IJGI 9(11):693. Radius method, verbatim: soundings "located inside the circle and the others will be eliminated (i.e., buffer radius)"; quotes NOAA's Nautical Chart Manual for the mm spacing figures (critical ≥ 6 mm / supporting ≥ 10 mm / fill 15–30 mm apart, supportive 40–100% of fill spacing). Read from the open-access PDF: https://www.mdpi.com/2220-9964/9/11/693 (PDF https://mdpi-res.com/d_attachment/ijgi/ijgi-09-00693/article_deploy/ijgi-09-00693-v2.pdf)
- ✅ **Kastrisios, C., Dyer, N., Nada, T., Calder, B. et al. (2025), "Sailing the Rough Seas of Automated ENC Compilation,"** ICA Advances 5:17. Sounding taxonomy (prime/least-depth/shoal/deep/supporting/fill), safety constraint, surface test, two-stage (hydrographic then cartographic) selection. https://ica-adv.copernicus.org/articles/5/17/2025/ica-adv-5-17-2025.pdf
- ✅ **Dyer, N., Kastrisios, C. & De Floriani, L. (2023), "Chart features, data quality, and scale in cartographic sounding selection from composite bathymetric data,"** Geo-spatial Information Science. Incorporates chart features (depth contours) and hydrographic-selection components into the cartographic selection process, and accounts for features "not considered for the sounding selection, such as wrecks, rocks, obstructions." Read from the author's open copy. DOI 10.1080/10095020.2023.2266222 · https://geovis.umiacs.io/publication/dyer-2023-chart/dyer-2023-chart.pdf
- ✅ **CCOM/UNH Joint Hydrographic Center — Hydrographic Sounding Selection project.** Definition of hydrographic selection ("shoal-biased and dense, yet manageable, subset"); radius/grid methods criticized as parameter-sensitive; label-based method + density-reduction tables (e.g. Charleston Harbor 221,494 → 1,345 soundings). https://www.ccom.unh.edu/research/research-areas/nautical-cartography/project-hydrographic-sounding-selection

## D. Point distribution & per-zoom thinning (technique)

- ✅ **Red Blob Games — jittered grid / Poisson disk / blue noise** (Amit Patel). A single jitter scalar can't be both isotropic and clump-free — jittered grid reads gridded _or_ clumps; Poisson-disk/blue-noise enforce a minimum spacing. https://www.redblobgames.com/x/1830-jittered-grid/
- ◑ **Mitchell's Best Candidate blue noise** (demofox) — deterministic, incremental, seedable. https://blog.demofox.org/2017/10/20/generating-blue-noise-sample-points-with-mitchells-best-candidate-algorithm/
- ◑ **Bridson fast Poisson-disk** (O(n) via active list + grid). https://github.com/martynafford/poisson-disc-distribution-bridson
- ◑ **R2 / golden-ratio quasirandom blue-noise sequence** (Martin Roberts) — deterministic _progressive_ sequence (add points in order to densify — a fit for densify-with-zoom). https://extremelearning.com.au/a-simple-method-to-construct-isotropic-quasirandom-blue-noise-point-sequences/
- ✅ **tippecanoe** — `--drop-densest-as-needed` (≈ radius method, spacing grows to fit), `-r`/`--drop-rate`, `-B`/`--base-zoom`, `-ah`/Hilbert order, **`-pi`/`--preserve-input-order`** (keep priority order → pre-sort shoalest-first). https://github.com/mapbox/tippecanoe
- ◑ **Felt — "How Tippecanoe makes your data look great at every scale."** Retained subset = uniform sample + points furthest from neighbours (blue-noise-ish); Hilbert order gives cheap spatial representativeness. https://felt.com/blog/tippecanoe-display-scale-precision
- ✅ **MapLibre Style Spec — symbols.** `symbol-sort-key` + `*-allow-overlap` collision behaviour; per-feature per-zoom `filter` (SCAMIN-style). https://maplibre.org/maplibre-style-spec/layers/
